# Brand Intelligence

## Identity scope

The job posting names **Quik Hire Staffing** as the recruiting intermediary. The associated public business identity is **Quick Hire / I Need A Quick Hire**. The end client is undisclosed, so the campaign does not invent client branding, strategy, products, leadership, architecture, or internal priorities.

- Public company site: `https://ineedaquickhire.com/`

## Visible company identity

The published campaign uses the clearly typeset public company name `Quick Hire`, paired immediately with `Candidate vision by Russell Dudek` / independent-candidate language. The official raster asset was not code-recreated. Direct connected publication in this repair supports UTF-8 source writes but did not provide a reliable binary-asset transport contract; therefore the mark is omitted rather than traced, redrawn, recolored, or replaced with a candidate pseudo-logo.

## Typography evidence and decision

The public identity uses a clean, accessible staffing-services presentation. The campaign implements a licensed/system-safe structural substitute: Lato/Segoe UI for readable body copy and PT Sans Narrow/Arial Narrow/Roboto Condensed behavior for compressed technical headings and labels. No proprietary font binaries are copied.

## Photography and imagery

No stock photography, client logos, product screenshots, employee imagery, or fabricated enterprise architecture is used. Employer recognition comes from the public company name and source-sampled color grammar. Candidate originality comes from the role-derived Enterprise AI Backplane.

## Geometry and motion

- Dense but legible technical surfaces
- Vertical continuity trace connecting six operating contracts
- Rectilinear cards, rules, and status states
- Motion shows continuity, failure isolation, hardening, and restored production state
- Reduced-motion mode removes looping movement while preserving all information and controls

## Artifact application

- Candidate vision: full violet/ink visual system and interactive Backplane
- Resume and cover letter: restrained violet rules, company name, shared candidate header
- Interview brief and entry plan: consulting-report treatment using the same tokens and contract vocabulary
- Backplane Review: printable worksheet using the six-contract model

## Independent-candidate distinction

Every public route and document identifies the work as Russell Dudek's independent candidate material. The campaign is not an official publication of Quick Hire, micro1, or the undisclosed end client.
