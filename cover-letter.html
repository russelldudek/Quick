Re: Member of Technical Staff, Enterprise AI

Dear Hiring Team,

The most consequential enterprise AI failures rarely begin with a model that cannot generate an answer. They begin when a technically capable model is placed into a system whose data is stale, runtime dependencies are brittle, authority boundaries are unclear, evaluation is too narrow, or post-launch ownership is diffuse. That is the operating problem behind this role—and the problem I would want to test with the client before assuming the title is primarily about model research.

My background is not a conventional five-year progression through foundation-model research or low-level PyTorch and JAX optimization. I would not present it that way. My verified strength is building and operating the surrounding system that makes emerging technology useful and governable: high-reliability engineering and process controls at Compunetics; launch, visibility, escalation, and standard work at Amazon; regulated autonomous-system and TensorFlow-based computer-vision concepts at ZeusVu; agentic workflow, retrieval, human-review, evaluation, and governance patterns through DudeWorth; and current AI-first operating transformation across robotics, machine vision, telemetry, ERP/Odoo, CRM, Jira, Slack, production, quality, support, and engineering issue flow at Vape-Jet.

That evidence points to a specific contribution: connect model behavior to the enterprise interfaces it depends on. I would make the outcome, context, model, runtime, human-authority, and evidence contracts explicit; identify the weakest production interface; harden it with the right tests, observability, fallbacks, escalation, and documentation; and turn the resulting proof into a reusable engineering pattern rather than another isolated demonstration.

The accompanying candidate vision makes that reasoning visible through an interactive Enterprise AI Backplane, a production fault drill, a balanced scorecard, and a first-90-days plan. The Backplane Review is a working artifact for examining a real system without pretending I know the client's undisclosed architecture or internal priorities.

I would welcome a direct fit conversation around the role's center of gravity. If the client needs a pure model researcher, there will be candidates with a more direct background. If the mandate is to make enterprise AI operable across models, APIs, controls, workflows, evidence, reliability, and ownership, I believe my adjacent technical and operating experience is credible, differentiated, and worth testing.

Carpe diem,
